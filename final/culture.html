History and Culture

Discover Korea's rich traditions, vibrant history, and cultural heritage that define its unique identity.

01 About Hanok

Experience Korean culture in a hanok, a traditional house known for its natural simplicity. With wooden structures and a front yard, hanoks offer stunning views. From traditional to contemporary, hanoks provide a tranquil escape, often with cultural experiences like tea ceremonies and cooking classes. While traditionally designed for a more sedentary lifestyle, modern hanoks often include amenities like beds for increased comfort.

02 About Hanbok

Hanbok, Korea's traditional clothing, is characterized by vibrant colors, flowing lines, and a distinctive silhouette. The hanbok, consisting of a jeogori jacket and a chima or baji skirt/pants, is often worn during special occasions. It represents Korea's rich cultural heritage and remains a symbol of national identity.

03 About Chuseok

Chuseok, South Korea's major harvest festival, celebrates family, tradition, and the bountiful harvest. Observed in September or early October, the festival involves ancestral reverence, traditional foods like songpyeon and hansik, family gatherings, and cultural performances.-17
